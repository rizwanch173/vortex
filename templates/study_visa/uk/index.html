Overview - UK Student Visa

The UK Student Visa - formally known as the Student Route - is required by most international students who wish to study in the United Kingdom at a higher education institution or further education college. If you are a citizen of a country outside the UK and you have been offered a place at a UKVI-licensed university or college, you will generally need this visa to enter and remain in the UK for the duration of your studies. The visa allows you to live and study in the UK, work a limited number of hours per week during term time, and travel in and out of the country while your visa remains valid.

Securing a UK Student Visa involves more than simply filling in a form. You need a Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS) from your institution, sufficient financial evidence, an approved English language qualification, and a correctly completed UKVI application - among other requirements. At Vortex Ease, we guide you through every single one of these stages. Our advisers know exactly what UKVI expects and how to present your application to the highest standard.

Who Is This For?

  • New international students who have received an offer of admission from a UK university or accredited college
  • Students already in the UK who need to extend their existing student visa to complete or continue their studies
  • Applicants in the UK on a different visa category (such as a Graduate or Skilled Worker visa) who wish to switch into the Student Route
  • Partners and children of eligible student visa holders who wish to apply as dependants alongside the main applicant

Eligibility Summary

To qualify for a UK Student Visa, you must meet several key criteria set by UKVI. Our team will assess your situation against each of these during your free consultation.

  • You must have an unconditional offer from a UK institution that holds a valid UKVI Sponsor Licence and is registered on the Student Sponsor Register
  • You must hold a valid CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) reference number issued by your institution, confirming the course details, fees, and your enrolment
  • You must demonstrate that you have sufficient funds to cover your tuition fees (as stated on your CAS) and your living costs for the duration of your course, in line with current UKVI financial requirements
  • You must meet the English language requirement - typically by providing a recognised SELT (Secure English Language Test) result such as IELTS Academic, TOEFL iBT, or PTE Academic, unless you are exempt

Key Documents - Quick Checklist

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months of validity beyond your intended course start date
  • CAS reference number issued by your UK institution
  • Bank statements or financial evidence showing required funds held for the stipulated period
  • English language test certificate (IELTS Academic, TOEFL iBT, PTE Academic, or equivalent)
  • University offer letter or confirmation of enrolment (if required alongside your CAS)

Timeline Overview

You can apply for your UK Student Visa up to 6 months before your course start date. If you are applying from outside the UK, you generally cannot apply more than 6 months in advance. Processing times vary - applicants outside the UK are typically advised to allow 3 weeks or more, though priority and super-priority services are available at additional cost. We always recommend applying as early as possible once your CAS has been issued. Note: processing times are subject to UKVI capacity and individual circumstances and are not guaranteed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most international students from outside the UK who intend to study at a UKVI-licensed university or college need a UK Student Visa. Citizens of the UK, Irish nationals, and people with settled or pre-settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme generally do not need to apply. If you are unsure, contact us for a free eligibility check.

A CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) is a unique reference number your institution issues once you have met the conditions of your offer. It contains key information about your course, fees, and sponsorship. You cannot apply for a UK Student Visa without it. Universities typically issue a CAS 6 to 10 weeks before the course start date, though timelines vary by institution.
